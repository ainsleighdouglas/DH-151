In this map, we compare the presence of data centers to carbon emissions around the world. Larger circles indicate a greater presence of data centers or higher emissions. In comparing this data, we can see that there is a slight correlation between data center quantities and emissions in a country. Countries with a large amount of data centers (namely the US and China) also emit large amounts. This is clearly not the only factor contributing to emissions, as illustrated by rising emissions in areas with fewer data centers, mainly in Africa. This was an expected finding, but the more important aspect of this representation is the revelation that a select few countries and regions are the hosts of the majority of data centers. This calls a number of questions about responsibility. Do we ask wealthier, industrialized countries to contribute more than others to emissions reduction, especially when it is less developed regions that bear the brunt of climate change challenges? Or do we continue as we are, knowing that more than half the world’s population now uses the internet? There are many implications of this data, and a great deal of important decisions to be made about what it means for our world. While we do not have the answers to these questions, we encourage you to think about how you see this issue being resolved most fairly, because it will only get worse with time.
