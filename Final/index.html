All internet activity is made possible through web hosting in data centers. Data centers are physical buildings that house the servers that host internet activity. Every time you use the internet, these servers power that activity. As you can imagine, it takes an immense amount of power to operate these servers safely and effectively to host the massive amount of internet activity that takes place every minute. Indeed, recent estimates hypothesize that data centers account for more than 3% of global electricity consumption and 2% of the world’s carbon emissions.


"In the US, data centres are responsible for 2% of the country’s electricity use"

This puts data centers on par with the airline industry in terms of their environmental impact (www.computerworld.com). However, there is still one big difference: our usage of the internet has grown exponentially, and will almost certainly continue to do so. We have only scratched the surface of what is possible with the computer power we can generate, and we can be sure that innovation will not stop. This leaves us a huge challenge that comes in two parts. First, we have to educate people on the true impact of the internet. We cannot live without it, but we have to be sure that we are aware of what living with it means for our future. Second, we have to develop mitigation techniques and eventually find suitable alternatives or innovations. This project aims to tackle the first of these issues. We cannot devise and implement a true solution to such a global issue without massive collective action efforts, but we can do our best to show that there is an issue that needs to be addressed. So we encourage you to read on, interact and engage with our maps and charts, and hopefully take a closer look at your own internet activity and impact.

Introduction

​

In today’s world, so many people in modernized countries are conscious of the impact they have on the environment. Airline travel, industrial factories, traffic, light pollution, and a wide variety of other human-made factors contribute to global carbon dioxide emissions and global warming. These factors are well-documented, and awareness of environmental issues is at a historic high, However, there is one impactful area that many people are less aware of: the internet. Public consensus has always seen the internet as a viable alternative to using paper books, notes, and records, but the benefit of the internet may not be as clear as it is assumed to be.
